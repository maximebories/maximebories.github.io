Work Experience

Career

  • 2025 → Today

    Lightning Location System Sensor Design

    Météorage

    Designing a from-scratch Lightning Location System (LLS) sensor prototype focused on ultra-precise time-of-arrival measurements. The core is a GNSS-disciplined timing and FPGA-based digital chain, ensuring deterministic clocks, counters, and data paths from acquisition to timestamping. Delivering a research-grade sensor enabling nanosecond-level timestamp accuracy from raw samples to lightning onset detection, with a complete end-to-end acquisition and control pipeline.

  • 2016 → 2023

    Algorithm Development and Validation

    Thales Alenia Space

    Developed and validated DSP algorithms for military-grade TT&C receivers and emitters onboard space satellites. From FSK/PSK modulation and frequency-hopping spread spectrum to synchronisation chains and secure transmission protocols, I carried the full lifecycle — design, simulation, coding, and testing — of custom modem models built to fly.

  • Summer 2014

    Software Developer Intern

    LIRYC — Electrophysiology and Heart Modeling Institute of Bordeaux

    Built the real-time software interface for an MRI-compatible 3D catheter localization system used in cardiac electrophysiology. Skin-surface electrode pairs induce oscillating electrical fields; voltage samples from the catheter tip are digitized and resolved via Fourier decomposition into live 3D coordinates. The Matlab-based interface handles calibration, acquisition, and real-time visualization — validated in vitro and in vivo, independent of MR scanning sequences.

Academic Projects

Research

  • End of 2015

    MSc Thesis — Underwater Vision

    Imperial College London

    Underwater cameras suffer from murky water — light gets absorbed and scattered, leaving images with low contrast, colour distortion, and near-field lighting artefacts. I explored ways to undo that damage in software, starting with classical image processing and moving to a physics-based model that estimates scene radiance and albedo to restore visibility in artificially lit underwater scenes.

  • End of 2014

    Second Year Research Project

    ENSEEIHT

    Detecting changes across multi-sensor satellite imagery — infrared, optical, and SAR. Applied wavelet-based preprocessing and multivariate statistical analysis to identify clusters of change between acquisitions.
